Common Wart
Verruca Vulgaris · Viral
📍 Hairline / Forehead edge
Cause: HPV (Human Papillomavirus) — skin-to-skin or surface contact
Looks like: Rough, raised bump with cauliflower-like or crusty surface. May have tiny black dots (clotted capillaries)
Viral Contagious Rough texture
Home treatment:
  • Salicylic acid gel/patch (17%) nightly — most effective OTC option
  • Apply, cover with tape for occlusion overnight
  • File dead skin with pumice after soaking
  • Apple cider vinegar soaked cotton pad (4–6 weeks)
  • ⚠️ Do NOT cut or pick — spreads HPV
⚪
Milia
Keratin Micro-Cysts · Non-infectious
📍 Forehead + Chin area
Cause: Trapped keratin under skin — dead cells unable to shed normally. Common with oily skin, heavy creams, or sun damage
Looks like: Tiny pearly white/yellow firm bumps, 1–2mm, no redness, no pus
Non-viral Not contagious Hard bump
Home treatment:
  • Exfoliate 2–3x/week with gentle AHA (glycolic acid)
  • Retinol cream at night — promotes cell turnover
  • Sunscreen daily (blocks sun-induced milia)
  • Switch to lightweight non-comedogenic moisturizer
  • ⚠️ Never squeeze — leaves scars, doesn't remove keratin
🔵
Molluscum Contagiosum
Poxvirus · Viral
📍 Near hairline (darker raised lesion)
Cause: Molluscum Contagiosum Virus (MCV) — direct contact or sharing towels/razors
Looks like: Dome-shaped, smooth, shiny bumps with a characteristic central dimple (umbilication). Can be skin-colored or slightly pink
Poxvirus Self-limiting Central dimple
Home treatment:
  • Tea tree oil (diluted 1:4 with coconut oil) applied daily
  • Colloidal silver or iodine topical application
  • Apple cider vinegar compress for 10 min daily
  • Avoid shaving over affected area — spreads virus
  • ⚠️ Resolves on its own in 6–18 months; dermatologist can freeze quickly
🔴
Comedones / Acne
Clogged Pores · Inflammatory
📍 Scattered forehead + chin
Cause: Excess sebum + dead cells blocking follicles. Worsened by sweat, heat (Delhi summer), and hormones
Looks like: Small red bumps, blackheads/whiteheads, occasionally pustules. The scattered small bumps on your forehead fit this pattern
Sebum-related Not contagious Common
Home treatment:
  • Benzoyl peroxide 2.5% wash or gel — kills bacteria
  • Salicylic acid 2% face wash twice daily
  • Niacinamide 10% serum — reduces sebum + inflammation
  • Ice cube on inflamed spots for 2–3 min
  • Avoid touching face — transfers oil and bacteria
